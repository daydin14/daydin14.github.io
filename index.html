My Journey dates back to high school when I initially learned to build computers. I worked with a team on several outdated hardware builds until I became full-fledged in creating my own to this day. Proceeding into the tech field, I started my coding career with some beginner courses in high school and that led me into my undergrad of Computer Science at Saint Joseph's University.

During my time at Saint Joseph's University, I was exposed to various coding languages, different architectures, algorithms, and more. I honestly struggled a lot to keep a balance of developing all of this simultaneously. Thankfully, a few willing peers of mine helped me along the way during some of my hardest times. It wasn't until after graduation that I had to keep persevering through tough learning.

I graduated May of 2020, in the heart of the COVID-19 pandemic. While the global pandemic locked up the world, I was trying my best to start my career. At the time, my resume was almost comical for the positions I thought I wanted. Eventually I started Amazon fulfillment work, organizing and managing high volumes of inventory, sorting packages, scanning QR codes. The commute became too much to the point where I was barely breaking even once gas prices surged. I spent six to seven months learning to work together as a team with many new friends within the facility. I was able to grow as a person in areas that I did not know. I learned how to effectively collaborate and contribute to a whole while developing strong personable skills.

Furthermore, I signed up for the General Assembly's Software Engineering Immersive course. My older brother had taken a data science course with General Assembly and highly recommended I do the same for software engineering. Throughout this 12-week course, I learned a plethora of techniques and coding languages. I can now proudly make new full-stack, frontend and backend applications using different databases.

After completing the class through General Assembly, my cohort nominated me as President as per one of the superlatives because of my leadership throughout the class material and fully inclusive collaboration and assistance while learning together.
